What I do

I love math and have spent many a dinner party talking about Klein bottles. I've worked with the National Museum of Mathematics (MoMath) and have written an award-winning research paper on the history & combinatorial applications of the Catalan number sequence.

I also love puns. My favorite, of course, being math puns. If you've ever wondered what the best thing about working at a math museum is, it's the puns. The gift shop at MoMath has shirts that say "Here's looking at Euclid" and ceramic ramekins with proofs on the bottom*.

I've worked the past two summers as a developer at JPMorgan Chase. I've had the opportunity to work on some really interesting projects, some more NDA-heavy than others. I've demoed to various Executive & Managing Directors, as well as the CIO of Corporate Technology Melissa Goldman.


I am graduating in May and am pursuing full-time opportunities for Software Development & Program Management roles.

A Y Combinator Startup School Member, Causmic is a startup that provides nonprofits with free technical help by pairing them with a team of computer science students. Students are afforded the opportunity to improve their skills, build their resume, and connect with Causmic's network of developers. An experienced industry mentor is provided to students to offer career advice and office hours to troubleshoot code.
